New Beginings.

An Orangutan Is Born

Mother orangutan gives birth to a baby orangtuan, weighing in at just 1.5 Kg. A mother orangutan can only have one baby every 8 years making it the only mammal with the longest time between births! Baby orangutans are among the most adorable and intelligent primates, with expressive faces and playful personalities.

Growing Up!

No Longer A Baby

When an orangtuan becomes a juvenile, his food changes and is now consuming solid nutrients. He starts becoming more independant and tends to travel alone a lot more often.Juveniles no longer share their mothers' night nests and begin building their own. The juveniles' nests remain close to their mothers and are often in the same tree.

Adolescent Era

Mommas Big Boy!

Once and Orangutan has completly gained independance from its mother, orangutans will often travel widely before settling in a permanent home range. Males tend to travel a lot further compare to their female counterparts.Female adolescents often stay with their mothers longer than males. If the mother has another baby, the female adolescent often helps her mother care for the infant, learning maternal behaviors

Adult

Always Mommas Baby

Females are considered adults once they have given birth to their first infant. Males are considered adults with the emergence of cheek pads, a throat pouch, and a developed long call.Adult male orangutans are intolerant of each other, and the encounter between two flanged adult males usually results in either aggression or avoidance. Adult orangutans have been documented to have gained self awarness.
